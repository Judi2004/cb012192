Kosgoda Sea Turtle Conservation Project

Kosgoda is one of the very few hatcheries where all the five species in the country make their nests.They are,

  • Olive Ridleys
  • Leatherbacks
  • Green Turtles
  • Loggerheads
  • Hawksbills

This hatchery strives to make great attempts to preserve and protect turtles by helping to enhance the process of hatching and ensuring safe survival of baby sea turtles after hatching. They also provide care and treatment for injured or sick adult sea turtles and then releasing them back into the sea. They also conduct workshops and programs creating awareness among foreign visitors and locals.

This project was initiated in 1981,by Dudley Perera, a native of Kosgoda and it is run by the Perera family. He began working with turtles straight out of school, funding the project with his own money.Dudley is educated in the conservation of sea animals, is very passionate and enthusiastic about his project. He loves to share his knowledge with both locals and tourists.

Victor Hasselblad sea turtle research and conservation center

This hatchery is the 1st turtle conservation center established in Sri Lanka in 1978.It was at a time when all 7 species of sea turtles were on the Endangered list.The famous Swedish camera maker Victor Hasselblad was a close friend of the highly renowned Sri Lankan bird photographer, Dr Upane de Zylva. Victor had made a donation to Dr. Zylva to be used in any form of conservation and research, in Sri Lanka. Dr.Zylva and the late Dr. Siri Wickremesinghe,who has similar interest in wildlife and nature, together decided that a turtle conservation project would be the best.

Nape, in Kosgoda was a favourite beach for the turtles to lay their eggs, and so it all fell into place. Thanks to Victor Hasselblad and Dr.Zylva and Dr. Wickremesinghe, the turtles have found a safe haven and they are increasing in numbers. It is a wonderful experience to see the hatchlings, once released on the beach, rushing to catch the first wave!

About us

We are here to share and give the community a full understanding about the sea turtles in the world and mainly focussing on the sri lankan sea turtles who are really in need. Sea turtles have been around for millions of years. Experts predict all of the different species may go extint unless successful sea turtle conservation programs are established and maintained.So lets spread this message and overcome the challenge. Contribute as much as possible to save the sea turtles.
