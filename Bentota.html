Bentota Sea Turtle Hatcheries

Sea Turtle Project

Turtle Hatcheries are quite common in down south. That is because down south beach is one of the major nesting beaches of Sri Lanka. The Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project is a popular attraction in Bentota for all travellers.This project aims at conserving and protecting the eggs from predators and other dangers.All of the 5 species of sea turtles in Sri Lanka can be seen in Bentota turtle hatchery as well. They are,

  • Olive Ridleys
  • Leatherbacks
  • Green Turtles
  • Loggerheads
  • Hawksbills

In Sea Turtle Project what they do is buying these turtle eggs from the people who collect them and protecting the eggs till they hatch. Normally it takes around six weeks for eggs to hatch. These eggs are kept under sand. These tiny hatchlings are normally kept in tanks for about 1-2 days and are released to the sea.

Sea Turtle Project does a great service by giving shelter to turtle eggs and taking care of them until they hatch. Also they give shelter to injured sea turtles as they cannot survive in the ocean alone. Volunteers can volunteer theirselves to take care of the injured and the baby sea turtles in this area.

The things you can do in here

  • Cleaning turtles and their tanks

  • Preparing food

  • Feeding the turtles

  • Assisting with beach cleaning

  • Maintaining hatcheries

  • Relocating eggs to protect them from predators

  • Releasing hatchlings

Volunteering yourselves will give this project a big help and it will help to spread the sea turtle conservation in sri Lanka and as well as in the whole world.Not only Sri Lanakans, foreigners are also capable of volunteering theirselves in this project.

About us

We are here to share and give the community a full understanding about the sea turtles in the world and mainly focussing on the sri lankan sea turtles who are really in need. Sea turtles have been around for millions of years. Experts predict all of the different species may go extint unless successful sea turtle conservation programs are established and maintained.So lets spread this message and overcome the challenge. Contribute as much as possible to save the sea turtles.
